TL; DR

We introduce VR-3R, a zero-shot framework that reconstructs high-quality 3D scenes robust to shadows and occlusions from a single RGB image by strategically integrating large-scale model priors.

Abstract

We present VR-3R, a zero-shot 3D scene reconstruction framework that enables immersive VR interaction from a single real-world image. The increasing demand for mixed-reality applications, including memory recollection and remote space exploration, highlights the importance of reconstructing 3D environments from arbitrary photographs. Despite advances in single-image 3D scene generation, existing methods remain limited by incomplete background recovery under occlusion and inaccurate object placement caused by monocular depth estimation. VR-3R addresses these challenges by integrating large-scale model priors to reconstruct high-quality 3D scenes without additional training. The framework incorporates VLM-based foreground–background separation and depth alignment with unprojected point clouds, achieving both scene-level consistency and detailed object recovery. Experiments on the 3D-FRONT dataset and in-the-wild real images demonstrate that VR-3R generates realistic backgrounds and accurate 3D models across diverse scenarios. We improve Chamfer Distance by over 23% and F-score by more than 6% at the object level, and achieves 12% and 18% gains in FID and FID-CLIP at the scene level. Our framework reconstructs interactive and immersive VR spaces from a single image, enabling practical deployment in real-world applications.

Method

Overview of the VR-3R pipeline. (a) Given a single input image, our system performs depth estimation, open-vocabulary segmentation, VLM-guided foreground–background separation, and super-resolution with amodal completion for 2D image understanding. (b) The foreground mask is then used to inpaint the background, and each object image is converted into 3D and integrated with the background. Finally, depth alignment is applied to reconstruct a compositional 3D scene from the single input image.
